Assignment 7 FlowMap of South American Bananas

I decided to map both net flows (with the choropleth map) and total flows (with the flow arrows). In the South, many of the flow lines overlap so I reduced the opacity to allow for the hidden lines to be seen. There are only 22 flow lines in this data so I did not have to limit the number. I used the half arrow design as it shows flow direction clearest. For the choropleth map, I chose a diverging color scheme and manually set the breaks so that the net exporter countries would be purple and net importer countries would be green. Since I was mapping two different variables with the two different symbologies, I decided to not add clutter by using the nodes to represent a variable. Instead, they are a semi-transparent, non-imposing white color.

In general, nothern coutries are net exporter countries while southern countries are net importers. Most flows run north to south. This implies that northern countries closer to the equator have a more suitable climate for growing bananas. Ecuador, despite its smaller size, exports the most bananas, while Argentina imports the most bananas.
