Gretchen Klock Undergraduate Student

I am an undergraduate student anticipating graduating in May 2024 from the University of Iowa with a B.S. in Environmental Planning and Policy with a GIS certificate and a history minor. I am currently researching the failed Chicago Crosstown Expressway for my honors history thesis.

I am studying the role of citizen activist groups, religious organizations, and the press in cancelling the Crosstown Expressway. Each of these groups were able to raise awareness for this issue and engage politicians by framing the Crosstown issue as a key election issue in both city and state elections.

I intend to frame my project within the wider history of urban freeway revolts (and with background information on the history of US highway politics and funding) that came after the “golden-age” of highway building after the 1956 Highway Infrastructure Act. The practice of building urban expressways into urban areas became more and more controversial during the 1960s and 1970s.

Additionally, I plan on studying the demographics of the affected area using US Census data and GIS.
